The Pearl of the Indian Ocean

Sri Lanka

Pearl of the Indian Ocean

Sri Lanka was formerly known as Ceylon to the world. Sri Lanka has a documented history of 3,000 years and a history of pre-historic human settlements found which age upto 125,000 years. The nickname "Pearl of the Indian Ocean" was given to this beautiful country due to it's geographical location and resources which captured interest from all over the world.

The location and resources of Sri Lanka made it a main part of the ancient Silk Road trade route, which made European countries want to capture this yet to be civilized land. The Portugese first captured it and next came the Dutch and finally the British captured Sri Lanka and made it a part of the British Colony from 1815 to 1948, and in 1948 the national movement for political independence arose and succeeded making Ceylon independent and was later renamed Sri Lanka.

Today Sri Lanka is home to millions of people of diverse sulture,languages and ethnicities.

Sri Lankan Cuisine

Explore new levels of Taste

Traditional local Foods

Hoppers

A batter mix with rice flour,coconut milk and sometimes a hint of suger. Fried in a small wok and swirled to even out the batter resulting in a thin Crunchy sheet surrounding a soft center. Choice of having it plain or with an egg in the centre with side dishes like onion sambol and chili.

Kiribath (Milk Rice)

A food usually made during local festivals or auspicious times. It is made by adding coconut milk and a pinch of salt while the rice is cooking so that it results in a sticky,firm but soft lump of rice which is evened out and sliced to slabs. Often served with chili and onion curry or Jaggery.

Pol Sambol (Coconut Sambol)

A Freshening and spicy delicious mix of grated coconut, onions, sliced chillies, chili-powder,squeezed lime,salt and pieces of maldive fish. All igredients are mixed up and combined in a bowl. This delicious mix goes well with what ever paired with rice,roti,hoppers it's your choice.

Traditional local Deserts

Aluwa

A Nutty delight which is mostly served during the Tamil and Sinhala New Year is one of the most loved desserts among the people. Usually prepared with palm treacle, rice flour and topped with cashews to get that nutty finish.

Dodol

Made with rice flour, coconut milk and juggery this hard jelly like delight is a favorite to many people. Most local stores have this stored in small packets so it's available any time of the year almost anywhere in the sourthen areas of the country.

Pani Walalu

A honey dipped sweet for those with a sweet tooth this will not dissapoint. The batter is compsed of flour, thick coconut milk, milk powder, water and salt which is quick fried and dipped in lots of honey before served.
